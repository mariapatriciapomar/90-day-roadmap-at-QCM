01
Days 1–30

Discover & Absorb

Immerse in QCM's solution portfolio, sales process, vendor ecosystem, and client base—earning credibility through rapid learning, not assumption.

The Strategic Story: The first 30 days are about building a foundation of trust. By deeply understanding QCM's products, vendor partnerships, pricing structures, and how the sales team operates, I position myself to add value quickly—turning from the "new hire" into a reliable technical resource the team can bring into client conversations with confidence.
PERFORMANCE GOALS

1Map QCM's Full Solution Portfolio

Document every product line, vendor partnership, and service offering—servers, storage, backup, networking, automation—understanding positioning, margins, and competitive differentiators.

2Shadow 5+ Active Sales Engagements

Sit in on live customer calls and proposal reviews to understand how deals are scoped, priced, and closed—identifying where a Solutions Architect adds the most value in the cycle.

3Audit Proposal & BOM Workflow

Review existing proposal templates, BOM processes, and quoting tools. Identify bottlenecks and quick wins to improve accuracy and turnaround time on solution proposals.

LEARNING GOALS

1Learn QCM's Vendor & Partner Ecosystem

Develop working knowledge of key vendor programs, partner portals, discount structures, and 3rd-party pricing processes for QCM's primary technology partners.

2Study QCM's Top Client Verticals

Research the industries QCM serves most—understanding their unique IT challenges, compliance requirements, and the operational processes that drive technology buying decisions.

3Deep-Dive on Backup, Recovery & DR Solutions

Build fluency in QCM's backup and disaster recovery offerings—understanding product configurations, sizing methodologies, and the competitive landscape of enterprise data protection.

INTERPERSONAL GOALS

11:1s with Every Sales Team Member

Meet individually with each sales rep to understand their accounts, pipeline, and where they most need architectural support—building trust through listening, not lecturing.

2Introduce Myself to Key Client Contacts

Join at least 3 customer meetings as observer or co-presenter—putting a face to the new Solutions Architect role and starting to build direct client rapport.

3Connect with Vendor Technical Resources

Establish relationships with vendor SEs and partner managers who will be critical allies when designing solutions and navigating complex configurations for customers.
